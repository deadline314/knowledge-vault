Squirl — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2026-06-30

Squirl is a browser extension that saves web pages and YouTube videos you choose to your own Google Drive and/or your computer. This policy explains what data Squirl processes.

What Squirl processes

  • Page content you clip. When you trigger a clip, Squirl reads that page's content (text, structure, and for YouTube the title, chapters, and subtitles) to build the file. This happens only on the tab you act on, only when you act.
  • Your Google account (optional). If you enable Google Drive, Squirl uses Google OAuth to create files in your Drive and reads your account email to show which account is connected.
  • Your settings. Preferences are stored locally via chrome.storage.

Where your data goes

  • To your Google Drive (only the folder you choose) and/or your local Downloads.
  • If you enable the optional “AI Desktop” integration, the clip is sent to the backend URL you provide and control.
  • Nowhere else. Squirl has no developer-operated server and performs no analytics.

What Squirl does NOT do

  • No tracking, advertising, or profiling.
  • No selling or sharing data with third parties.
  • No background collection; extraction runs only on a page you actively clip.
  • No remote code execution.

Data retention

Squirl stores only your settings, locally, until you change/remove them or uninstall. Clipped files live in your Drive/computer. The Google OAuth token is managed by Chrome/Google and can be revoked at myaccount.google.com/permissions.

Google API Services / Limited Use

Squirl's use of information received from Google APIs adheres to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements. Drive access uses the drive.file scope, limiting Squirl to files it creates.
